The orgins of the Toyota Landcruiser

In 1951, during the Korean war, Toyota was commissioned by the United States Government to create trucks for military use fitting within Willy's specifications. This Toyota BJ or Jeep BJ as it was called later became the official patrol car for the National Police Agency of Japan. After two years in 1953 the Toyota Jeep BJ was produced in regular production instead of only being produced for small orders and contracts. In 1954 the name "Land Cruiser" was attached to the vehicle and was the beginning a lengthy legacy of ruggedness and reliability.

The Land Cruiser Hits The International Market

In 1954 Toyota released the J20, the successor to the BJ designed with civillian use in mind. The J20 was a more refined version of the BJ, with a more comfortable interior and improved handling. The J20 was the first Land Cruiser to be sold outside of Japan, with exports to Australia. This is also the first time Toyota began manufacturing outside of Japan, with a factory in Brazil producing the J20 or the Toyota Bandeirante for the South American market. The J20 was followed by the J30 in 1955, which featured a more powerful engine and improved suspension. The J30 was a strange model only being produced for around 9 months. The purpose of this series seemed to be improved cabin size with a different chassis. The J40 is where the Land Cruiser began to establish itself as a global icon, with a design that emphasized durability and off-road capability.

Modern Changes to the Land Cruiser

In the early 2000s many changes small and large were done to the Land cruiser platform one being the modernization of the exterior design. Leaving the older more boxy design behind as well as the rebadging of trucks starting with the 80 series model around the middle of it's production years. In 2007 the Land Cruiser 200 series was released with a more luxurious interior and a more powerful engine. The 200 series was designed to be a more comfortable and refined vehicle, while still maintaining the off-road capability that the Land Cruiser is known for. In 2021 the Land Cruiser 300 series was released with a new platform and a new engine. The 300 series is designed to be more fuel efficient and environmentally friendly, while still maintaining the off-road capability that the Land Cruiser is known for.

The Toyota Landcruiser in the modern era

In 2024 Toyota reintroduced the Land Cruiser 70 series with a modern take. This car revives the classic look of the 70 series but with all the modern luxuries expected in 2024. 2024 was also the release year for the Land Cruiser J250 to become a continuation of the Land Cruiser Prado with the power of the 300 series, a modern Land Cruiser. This is the Land Cruiser's most recent development.
